DH Projects

Student projects

These projects were executed as part of the Digital Humanities Program at the University of Graz

I modeled a list of the top 50 best films of the genre film noir. The list I based my model on stems from the publisher Taschen, which published a book describing various themes and motifs of film noir. The original list served to introduce film noir as a cinematic genre by compiling its most influential films. The purpose of the database was to analyze the distribution of aspects like the country in which the film was created or how many movies were created outside of the United States. In brief, this project aimed at analyzing the diversity and complexity of Taschen’s canon.

I encoded an episode of a cartoon called "Gravity Falls" according to TEI. The encoded text is based on the automated transcript function on Youtube and a fan-made transcript. In my encoding, I treated the text as a performance text (TEI Module 7) and thus used elements like <sp>, <speaker>, <p>, <view>, <camera> and <stage>.

I worked with a dataset provided by Princeton's "Shakespeare and Company Project." The project deals with Sylvia Beach's bookstore "Shakespeare and Company" founded in Paris in 1919. The still-existent lending library records were digitized by Princeton as CSV files. The dataset itself consists of information about 6,020 books and other items in fields like book URL, title, author, borrow count, and purchase count.My aim is to answer questions such as
What are the top 20 most borrowed books?
What is the range of the publication years?
How many books does the dataset include?
